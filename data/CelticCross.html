Celtic Cross

Memorial Chapel

 

Studio:  Picketts of Leeds  1948 

Dedication:  SIDNEY BAKER WHELDON - CHURCHWARDEN 1900-1921

The beautiful worked tapestry of a celtic cross, with its rich variety of colours, surrounded by tongues fire, is the focal point of this holy place and complements the Celtic cross in the modern stained window opposite.

We are reminded of St Patrick, St Columba, St Aidan and St Cuthbert; of the holy islands of Iona and Lindisfarne through whom, and through which, the Holy Spirit worked to bring the Good News of Jesus Christ to northern Britain some time before the arrival of Paulinus, at York in 625.

Notes on Stained and Painted Glass by The Revd. Peter Marshall, B.A.(Vicar 1983-1998) and Professor David Davies
